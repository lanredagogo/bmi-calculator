Limitation of BMI

Although BMI is often a pratical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suited for every person. Specific group should carefully consider their BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measurement may not be benefit may not beneficial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat conposition of boys and girls vary with age. consequently, a childs age and gender are considered when evaluating thier BMI.

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregnancy

Expectant mother experience weight gain due to their growing baby. maintaing a pre-pregnancy BMI is advisable to minise health risk for both mother and child.

Race

Certain health concerns may affect individuals of some black and asian origin at lower BMI than others. To learn more, it is adviced to discuss this with your Gp or practice nurse.
